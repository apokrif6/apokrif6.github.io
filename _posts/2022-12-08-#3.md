...or how **Kena: Bridge of Spirits** brought back aid kits on locations into trend.

Old games were extremely linear, so designers placed heal boxes at levels depending on idea of level. Boxes were placed in available spaces, to player could pick it and heal some HP in fight.
One of main disadvantages this system - it's not universal. Some players could pass level without picking any box, and some players needed in more. Your duty, as a designer, think about every player. Even about bad player.
Game which did on first steps in way to prove this system was _Half Life_. It used system, which checked your HP level when you destroy wood boxes. And if you were at low HP, it was higher chance to get an aid kit.
This system was great, imho.

Today's healing system allow player play their own style. If you wanna play agressive - just craft more heal items and let's go. You prefer stealth? You can ignore aids and focus on crafting another items.
Eestus from _Dark Souls_, herbs from _Horizon_, even cannibalism from _Fallout_ - all this mechanics which allow you do what you, do when you want, and **heal** when you want.

But what if we could combine those twos?

Allow me to introduce, healing flower from _Kena_:

![]({{BASE_PATH}}/assets/images/3.jpg)

In this game you are friend with Rot. Small cute creatures. They give power to you, and you can use different spells or interact with world in another way. For example you can use your Rot point to spawn huge hammer and damage everybody in area.
And also you can heal. You can't use your ability to heal in anytime, you can heal only by interacting with healing flowers. And, of course, it costs rot points.

![]({{BASE_PATH}}/assets/gifs/3.gif)

And amount of those flowers is defined for level. Yeah, it means that you have limited amount of heals per _encounter_. Sound like old-like system, isn't it? For me, yes it is.
But Kena is relatively new game, so structure and design of whole game and each encounter is new.
In practice, this game wanna be hard. And it is. Don't be fooled by art style. Game is such hard, as cute. And limited amount of healing only works for her.

At first, you shouldn't think about heals. There is no need to but or craft something. You sure that there will be healings near next encounter and you will in safe.

You can't get healing accidentally and lose one. There is no collider to overlap. There is system, where you need to aim, choose your target, and use spell. There is no chance to do it accidentally. I hope...

And last main thing, it's improving skills. Nowadays designers are much better than time ago. They know what and how players do. They know our needs and capabilities.
So they are sure, that you need only two healing to kill this boss. If you can't - you should go through loop, and improve your skill.

This system allow you play like you want, you don't need healing if you playing with bow or uses shield so much. But at the same time it's system which allow you heal only when you really need, and when you waighed all props and cons.
And for me, it's really looks like old healing box in modern game system. And I like it so much.
