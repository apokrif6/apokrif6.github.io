One of the hardest things in game design - is to create a useful game mechanic. Useful and usable mechanic.
It's really hard to create mechanic, which players will be satisfied. They should learn how to deal with it, so they must to invest their time and effort. That means, that your mechanic should be enough usable to players do this.
But how you can show, that your mechanic is usable? Well, you can teach how to use it, and players will decide by themself.
There are different ways to teach, but today I would like to tell about one of my favourite - field trainings.

**Kena: Bridge of Spirits** has done good work with creating a improving skills loop.

![]({{BASE_PATH}}/assets/images/4.jpg)

I will show you, what I mean describing one of bosses - __Wood Knight__, but also give another examples.

- First of all, we meet Rusu, and get an opportunity to use a bow.
- In this step we have small mission - hit few targets with our new bow. It's a small challenge.
- There is my favourite part - fight with boss. Our boss is __Wood Knight__ with huge sword.
There we can find all good things of Kena's boss design. Hassle starts with range mobs which are spawned od trees based on arena (example of good level design btw). We should hit them only one way - with our new bow.

![]({{BASE_PATH}}/assets/gifs/4_1.gif)

It's a trick, which makes us think: "Hey, bro, bow could be cool in this fight, isn't it?"
Amount of HP of this boss is kinda larger than the previous ones. So when you try to hit this one, you could be suprised of his HP. But then, you will notice some yellow stones stucked in his body. And you will remember, that now you have bow. And you will try to hit one of those stone, and if you will - you'll make a lot of damage.
Nice, you have found a way to kill this one boss. Of course, you can kill him by "traditional way". But it will be extremely harder and longer. Game doesn't reward this. But game reward you when you use your new skill.

![]({{BASE_PATH}}/assets/gifs/4_2.gif)

Game tell you how to kill boss with your new ability. Now you have satisfaction for winning this fight and you know how to use your new skill.
- At last, you can have some fun with your new ability!

And this loop is common in Kena. You should understand how to use shield, to beat __Sproout__, you should learn how to use rot ability to kill __Kappa__ and you should throw your bombs to win fight with __Vine Knight__.
I'm not exaggerating if say that almost every boss in __Kena__ is a traning room, where you should to understand how to do something, for leave this room.
