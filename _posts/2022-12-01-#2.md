Game metrics care a foundation of competent level design. They define sizes, distances and timings. Expierence directly depends on metrics.

How high we can jump, how many boxes for hide there are, how much time you spend to run from point A to point B, how far interactive button is after obstacle.

All this is so important, and creates something, that we call _game flow_. Have you ever had a feeling, that game fell so slow, or so dynamic? It's because metrics are not so good.

But today I would like to tell about good metrics. In Hollow Knight, which is metroidvania, we have so many platforms where we can move and jump. And of course, you should jump, to cross the abyss beetwen some platforms.
And at one of start locations we can find item **Mothwing Cloak**. It's kinda _dash_, so you can move faster, or you can get to places where you weren't. And of course, you can cross small distances beetwen platforms. When you are in dash, gravity don't working on you, so literally can walk on air.

![]({{BASE_PATH}}/assets/gifs/2_1.gif)

In practice, you will overuse this spell, because it's so enjoyable to use. And in long _platforming session_, jumping will interwine with dashing, and will create infinite jump session. Using of combination of those mechanics is so intuitive, that platforming in this game is such good expierence.
It's works as it should, thanks to competent metrics. Correct ratio of distance between platforms and distance of dash affects so positive on your game expierence. And this combinations allows you to jump without reasoning when to jump.
